I started holding silat in the first semester because I was very interested in self-defense training. Besides, I was inspired by my male cousin because he made a silat presentation at his sister's wedding. I used to be a techwando member, but since our teacher moved to a another place, we decided to stop because no one could send me there all weekend.

The silat I follow is the Cekak silat, which is a combat silat. We're trained to 99% wait for the attack of the 1% attack, all our movements according to human will or in other words according to the movement of the prayer and we have 4 methods and 21 buah movements that different every level. If you curious about what kind 21 buah we learn, you click this "Matapelajaran Seni Silat Cekak Malaysia"

When I got into the silat, I thought we were gonna only have a workout, but I was wrong. The association is more than just teaching us to defend ourselves in our soft skills by holding a variety of programs either formal or casual. I love the teachers and the other silatists because they're like my family. I learned a lot of new skills when I joined PSSCM.
